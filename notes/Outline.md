# **ArtWork – Concept Outline**

**Highlights**

* Interactive decision-support for discovering and selecting artworks.  
* Explores lighting and situatedness: artworks can be viewed under different lighting contexts and imagined within lived spaces.  
* Investigates interface design informed by circadian principles and gradual environmental change, without making medical claims.  
* Supports decision pacing: users can browse quickly or pause to explore additional context when uncertain.  
* Prioritises user understanding over persuasion or sales optimisation.  
* Future directions include ecological materials, provenance, AI-assisted descriptions, and richer contextual information.  
* Designed for both individual buyers and professional users such as interior designers, hotels, cafés, and public spaces.  
* Makes art discovery more accessible for people who are unsure how to evaluate or choose artworks.

## **Current Prototype**

ArtWork is a web interface for exploring artworks through interactive filters, colour relationships, lighting exploration and contextual viewing. Rather than relying only on search or recommendation algorithms, it helps people explore artworks in ways that reflect where and how they may ultimately be displayed.

## **Research Direction**

How can interactive systems support context-aware decision-making without replacing human judgement? The interface provides information and perspectives while leaving the final decision with the user.

## **Design Principles**

\- Decision pacing: adapt to user uncertainty rather than forcing everyone through the same process.  
\- Meaningful pause: reveal relevant information when it can improve a decision.  
\- Context over persuasion: optimise for understanding rather than conversion.  
\- Lighting as context: explore how changing light alters perception of artworks.  
\- Systems thinking: connect artwork, viewer, room, materials, artist and environment.

## **Future Directions**

Potential developments include AI-assisted descriptions and themes, ecological and material provenance, reused materials, ethical metrics, accessibility, maintenance information, richer lighting simulations and, as a longer-term research topic, financial history and valuation.

## **Why Art?**

Art is a rich yet relatively low-risk domain for studying decision support. It combines aesthetic, spatial, emotional and practical considerations, making it an excellent first case study before extending similar principles to other kinds of products and decisions.
