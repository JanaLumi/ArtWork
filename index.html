Art@Work programme

Artists belong in the rooms where the future is being built.

The problem

Engineering answers what and how. Artists answer why it matters in human terms. In Finland, where engineers hold significant cultural authority, that gap is visible — and costly. Art that works becomes invisible. Only noticed when it's gone.

The model

Art@Work embeds artists in engineering and science companies as storytellers and visual communicators — with the same access, status, and professional standing as other contributors. Inspired by NASA's Artist in Residence programme, where artists hold equivalent clearance to scientists.

For companies

Living, contemporary work made by people in your city, your time. Not stock imagery, not AI generation. An artist who understands your work and can make it legible to the world outside your walls.

For artists

Professional parity, not patronage. Paid at the rate of a consultant or specialist. Access to environments, networks, and materials that expand your practice — not just a residency stipend and a corner to work in.

About

Jane & Lumi / Jana Lumi

Art@Work is a programme developed by Jana Lumi — designer, researcher, and artist based in Tampere. The work sits at the intersection of systems thinking, human-technology interaction, and the question of what gets counted as valuable in working life.

The paintings in this archive were made on MDF, hardboard, and reclaimed futon canvas. Some glow under ultraviolet light. All are available for spaces that want something present and made by hand.
