**System Journey — the story of what happens**
You have work to add. Maybe a folder of images and a spreadsheet ready to go. Maybe just one piece you want to put in carefully. You choose how to enter — batch or single. Either way, nothing leaves your machine.
The ingestion script wakes up. This is the machine working — no intelligence, just rules executing. It reads each image three ways: it measures the mathematical complexity of what's in the frame, it maps the ratio of warm to cool colour, it appends these as a clean line in your spreadsheet. Automatic. Deterministic. Fast.
Then the AI becomes available — if you want it. Not assumed. You decide, field by field, what you'd like it to suggest. A title. Descriptive tags. Dominant colours. A price indication based on comparable work. You look at what it offers and take what's useful. Edit what isn't. Reject it entirely if you prefer. The AI is another presence in the room, not an authority.
Then comes the part only you can do.
You enter the material truth of the piece: what it's made of, where those materials came from, how far it would need to travel, what the gallery takes. This isn't data entry. This is the record of choices you made before anyone else saw the work — the actual physical history of the object. The machine waited for this. The AI couldn't infer it. It exists in the system because you put it there.
The machine compiles everything into a single structured file. Local. Static. Yours.
Now the AI reads the Planetary Threshold — not to pass or fail the piece, but to make the tensions visible. Shipping distance against material source. Commercial weight against ecological cost. Synthetic pigments against recycled ground. These don't cancel out neatly. They sit in relation to each other, unresolved, as a field you can see and navigate. This is a boundary object — something different people read differently, without needing to agree on a single interpretation.
You look at where your piece sits. You decide whether to proceed.
If you do, the machine takes over again. The artwork surfaces in the discovery interface — filtered, contextualised, its tensions legible to anyone who wants to look. Not promoted. Not pushed. Made findable, for someone genuinely looking.
